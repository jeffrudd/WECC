The Problem

The west end of Toronto curling community is seriously underserved.

  • There are only 11 sheets of ice for the western 1/3 of Toronto.

  • As of April 2020, there will be only 5 sheets (High Park Curling Club).

  • The model of golf-curling operations is failing (3 clubs closed in 2018-2019).

  • A large number of displaced curlers in west Toronto can't find places to curl.


The Vision


To create a premier event centre that provides curling and other services the whole west Toronto community can enjoy. Initially, the focus would be on accomidating 1000-1500 curlers (including members & rentals). This future site would be suitable for hosting major events with television exposure.

The facility would serve the community in the following ways:

  • All members of our diverse community will be welcome.

  • All forms of adapted curling would be included. For example wheelchair, Special Olympics, vision impaired, and others.

  • Year-round use including off-season with activities such as pickleball, ball hockey, celebrations, summer camps, and community events.


The WECC (West End Curling Committee)


A group formed in the winter of 2019 consisting of concerned and passionate west end curlers dedicated to enabling the curling facility West Toronto deserves.
