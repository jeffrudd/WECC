On The Issues

Etobicoke is Dramatically Under Serviced in Meeting the Demand for Curling

The fundamental issue is that Etobicoke is dramatically under serviced in meeting the demand for curling. There is currently only one curling facility (at St. Georges Golf & Country Club) for all of Etobicoke and as of April 2020 that curling facility will be closed. This isn't due to a lack of demand for curling but rather a shift in club direction to make St. Georges golf-centric. This is a major blow to our community leaving no curling facilities in Etobicoke as of April.

A similar fate befell the curling facility at the Weston Golf & Country Club the fall of 2018.

The loss of these two curling facilities displaces approximately a thousand curlers of all kinds including youth curlers, senior curlers, wheelchair curlers, Special Olympics curlers, curlers in church groups, Toronto Sports and Social Club curlers, teacher's league curlers, numerous one-time rentals from businesses and more. All these curlers will have to leave Etobicoke to follow their passion for curling. Curling is too important to the community in Etobicoke to let it wither and die.

Meanwhile, there are seven curling facilities east of Avenue Road including three City owned ones which are oversubscirbed and turning away potential curlers. There is a clear call to do better for the community in Etobicoke.

To answer that call, we need the support of the City of Toronto through its councillors and the Parks, Forest and Recreation Department. We also need the strong voices of the thousands of those whole love curling in Etobicoke and even those who have never curled but don't want to lose the chance to try it.
